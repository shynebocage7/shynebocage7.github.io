My Background

I was born in Oahu, Hawaii and lived there for 6 years. My father got a job opportunity in California so we moved to Tracy, where I spent the rest of my life. When I was around 11 years old, I started to play volleyball in middle school and on a club team. My Sophomore year was when my father wanted me to think about possible career and I chose to become a pilot. I wanted to take a break off of playing volleyball and did Shot Put and Discus for the Track and Field team in the Spring.

Taking this break allowed me to have some free time and go to my flying lessons at a municipal airport near my house. I was planning on becoming a pilot and going into the Air Force or to the Embry Riddle Aeronautical University in Florida. I was interested for a while, but I couldn't stick with new activities much when I was younger and I just stopped going. My eyesight got worse over time, so it was like my body was telling me that becoming a pilot wasn't for me.

Then my Junior year, I thought of some hobbies that I have or things that come naturally to me and told my father that I wanted to become a chef. I started cooking when I was about 10 years old and have loved it ever since. My father thought that it would be better for me to go to college and get a degree in order for me to become a restaurant owner and also be a chef. I thought it was a great idea because it gives me a better chance at succeeding and getting paid more for what I do and love. Now, I am working to begin this goal of mine to be a chef and become my own boss one day.
